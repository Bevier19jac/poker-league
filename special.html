What is the Mid-Season Tournament?
🃏
Different Format
Unlike regular Thursday night games, the Mid-Season Tournament is a dedicated Saturday event with a longer blind structure, deeper starting stacks, and more time to play real poker. Think of it as the league's mini-main event.
⭐
Why It Matters
Points earned here count toward the Season 20 leaderboard, affecting your starting chip count at the Season Final. A deep run here can move you significantly up the standings — or protect your position.
📅
Saturday Schedule
Being on a Saturday means no weeknight rush. Expect a noon-ish start, proper breaks, and a relaxed pace that lets the cards breathe. Contact Tod for the confirmed start time and final details.

Format & Rules
01
Texas Hold'em — No Limit. Same game as always, deeper stacks and a slower blind structure to reward skill over luck.
02
APL Points. Points earned at the Mid-Season Tournament count toward the Season 20 standings just like a regular event — same multipliers, same system.
03
Starting Stacks. Starting stacks may be scaled differently than regular Thursday events. Final stack sizes will be confirmed by Tod before the event.
04
Blind Structure. Extended levels — longer than Thursday nights. Expect more play and fewer gamble-shove situations at the start. Exact levels TBD
05
Breaks. Structured breaks between levels, including a longer dinner/food break. Saturday means we have all day.
06
Registration. RSVP on the home page. Contact Tod directly for any questions. Space may be limited — commit early.
